Legal Framework of the Overseas Employment Corporation (OEC)

Statutory Status

The Overseas Employment Corporation (OEC) is a public sector company wholly owned by the Government of Pakistan. It operates under:

  1. Companies Act, 1913 (now Companies Act, 2017) – OEC is incorporated as a legal entity under this act.
  2. Emigration Ordinance, 1979 – The foundational law governing all overseas employment activities in Pakistan.
  3. Emigration Rules, 1979 (Updated 2023) – Framework for managing documentation, protector processes, and recruiter obligations.

Regulatory Oversight

OEC functions under the umbrella of the:

  • Ministry of Overseas Pakistanis & Human Resource Development (OP&HRD)
  • In collaboration with:
    • Bureau of Emigration & Overseas Employment (BEOE)
    • Protectorate of Emigrants Offices
    • Federal Investigation Agency (FIA) for anti-trafficking compliance
    • Ministry of Foreign Affairs and relevant embassies for attestation and visa coordination

Licensing & Exclusivity

  • OEC is exempt from licensing under the Emigration Ordinance 1979.
  • It is the only organization authorized by the Federal Government to carry out recruitment through G2G (Government-to-Government) and G2Private channels without intermediary license requirements.

Public Protection Measures

  • No Agent Policy: OEC does not work through private agents or sub-contractors
  • Service Fee Transparency: All charges are officially approved and displayed publicly
  • Dispute Resolution: Grievance redressal system and direct coordination with foreign employers
