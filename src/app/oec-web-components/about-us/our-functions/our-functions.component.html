Core Roles & Responsibilities of the Overseas Employment Corporation (OEC)

Strategic Role

As Pakistan’s only government-owned overseas recruitment agency, OEC operates with the mandate to manage, regulate, and facilitate the legal export of Pakistani manpower to foreign countries.

Key Functions by Category

  • ✅ 1. Demand Management
    • Receiving job demands directly from foreign governments and verified private employers
    • Scrutinizing and authenticating employer credentials through diplomatic or ministry channels
    • Preparing and publishing advertisements through digital and print media
  • ✅ 2. Job Seeker Registration & Processing
    • Facilitating online registration of applicants via official portals
    • Managing data-driven shortlisting based on employer criteria
    • Verifying academic, professional, and legal documents
    • Organizing employer-led interviews (online or physical)
  • ✅ 3. Post-Selection Facilitation
    • Guiding selected applicants through medical, visa, and protector procedures
    • Coordinating with:
      • Embassies
      • Protector of Emigrants
      • MOFA (for attestation)
      • Police for clearance certificates
    • Providing pre-departure orientation sessions
  • ✅ 4. Recruitment Projects Management
    • Executing Government-to-Government (G2G) recruitment schemes (e.g., Korea EPS, KSA MNGHA, Italy Nurses)
    • Partnering with foreign institutions for demand-based hiring
    • Handling language training and examination (where required)
  • ✅ 5. Digital System Operations
    • Operating secure online job portals and applicant dashboards
    • Managing dynamic validator teams to ensure document compliance
    • Developing AI-supported job matching (future module)
  • ✅ 6. Stakeholder Coordination
    • Working closely with:
      • Ministry of OP&HRD
      • BEOE
      • NAVTTC & TEVTA (for training alignment)
      • International employers and embassies
  • ✅ 7. Grievance Redressal & Worker Welfare
    • Receiving and resolving complaints via online form, call, or email
    • Maintaining contact with deployed workers for post-selection support
    • Intervening in case of contract violation or abuse (in liaison with missions)
  • ✅ 8. Corporate & Financial Compliance
    • Ensuring compliance with PPRA Rules, Emigration Ordinance 1979, and audit procedures
    • Managing internal budgeting and project-based financial transactions
    • Preparing annual performance and deployment reports
