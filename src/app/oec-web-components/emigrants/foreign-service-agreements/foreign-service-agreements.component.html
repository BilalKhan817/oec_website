Legal Contracts for Safe and Transparent Overseas Employment

What is a Foreign Service Agreement?

A Foreign Service Agreement is a legally binding employment contract signed between a Pakistani worker and a verified foreign employer — facilitated and verified by the Overseas Employment Corporation (OEC). It outlines all employment terms and ensures worker protection under both Pakistani emigration law and host country labor laws.

Key Objectives

  • ✅ To ensure legal protection of Pakistani workers abroad
  • ✅ To define terms and conditions of employment clearly
  • ✅ To prevent exploitation, fraud, or contract substitution
  • ✅ To provide a reference for dispute resolution

Contract Contents

Each agreement includes:

Clause
Details
Job Title & Role
Designation, duties, department
Salary & Allowances
Monthly wage, overtime, bonuses
Accommodation
Free/shared housing (if provided)
Working Hours
Weekly schedule, rest days
Contract Duration
Typically 1–2 years, renewable
Medical & Insurance
Employer-paid coverage (if included)
Termination Clause
Grounds and procedures for ending contract
Leave & Benefits
Annual leave, sick leave, air ticket entitlements

Verification & Attestation

Before departure:

  • 📋 The agreement must be verified by OEC
  • 📋 Attested by the Embassy of the host country (if applicable)
  • 📋 Signed by both employer and employee in English + local language

Worker Rights Under Contract

  • ✅ No change in salary or job role without consent
  • ✅ No deduction beyond what is mentioned
  • ✅ Protection from overwork or abuse
  • ✅ Legal right to file complaint via OEC or embassy

Dispute Resolution

In case of dispute:

  • ⚖️ Worker must notify OEC or relevant embassy
  • ⚖️ OEC liaises with the employer, mission, or local court
  • ⚖️ Emergency repatriation or mediation can be arranged if needed
